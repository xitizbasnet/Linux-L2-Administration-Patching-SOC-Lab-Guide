# Task 1.1 – Shift Roster Setup & SLA Management

## Overview

This task outlines the standard operating procedures for Linux L2 Engineers during the beginning of each shift. It covers shift roster verification, Service Level Agreement (SLA) awareness, system readiness validation, incident review, and communication responsibilities to ensure uninterrupted 24×7 operational support.

---

## 🎯 Objective

Before assuming operational responsibilities, every engineer must:

* Review and confirm the assigned shift schedule.
* Understand and comply with SLA/OLA requirements.
* Verify access to all required systems and monitoring tools.
* Review ongoing incidents and monitoring alerts.
* Complete shift handover and communicate shift readiness.

---

## 📌 Key Responsibilities

| Responsibility                    | Description                                                                                                         |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 📅 **Weekend & Holiday Coverage** | Ensure continuous support during weekends and public holidays according to the approved shift roster.               |
| 🤝 **Shift Handover**             | Perform a complete knowledge transfer between outgoing and incoming engineers before taking ownership of the shift. |
| ⏱️ **SLA Monitoring**             | Monitor incident response and resolution times to ensure SLA compliance throughout the shift.                       |

---

# 1. Review the Shift Roster

Obtain the latest weekly or monthly shift roster from your **Team Lead** or **NOC Manager**.

### Verify Your Assigned Shift

| Shift            | Working Hours     |
| ---------------- | ----------------- |
| 🌅 Day Shift     | **06:00 – 14:00** |
| 🌆 Evening Shift | **14:00 – 22:00** |
| 🌙 Night Shift   | **22:00 – 06:00** |

### Required Actions

* Review your assigned working schedule.
* Mark all weekend and holiday shifts in the team's shared calendar (Google Calendar or Microsoft Outlook).
* Confirm that all three operational shifts have assigned engineers.
* Ensure no shift is left without an approved replacement or backup engineer.

> **Note**
>
> Continuous coverage is essential for maintaining 24×7 service availability.

---

# 2. Understand the SLA (Service Level Agreement)

Review the **Service Level Agreement (SLA)** and **Operational Level Agreement (OLA)** provided by your client or manager before handling incidents.

## SLA Response and Resolution Targets

| Priority  | Severity | Initial Response      | Target Resolution |
| --------- | -------- | --------------------- | ----------------- |
| 🔴 **P1** | Critical | Within **15 minutes** | **2–4 hours**     |
| 🟠 **P2** | High     | Within **30 minutes** | **4–8 hours**     |
| 🟡 **P3** | Medium   | Within **1 hour**     | **24 hours**      |
| 🟢 **P4** | Low      | Within **4 hours**    | **72 hours**      |

### Important

> **⚠️ SLA breaches must be escalated immediately to the Shift Lead.**

Every SLA breach must be:

* Documented in the incident ticket.
* Reported to the Shift Lead.
* Escalated according to the organization's escalation procedure.

# 3. Verify System Readiness

Log in to the jump server (Bastion Host) at least **15 minutes before your scheduled shift**.

## Bastion Login

```bash
ssh ops_engineer@bastion.company.internal
```

## Verify Access to Required Tools

Confirm successful access to all monitoring platforms:

* SolarWinds
* Nagios
* Zabbix
* Grafana

## Verify VPN Connectivity

Ensure your VPN connection is active and stable before accessing internal systems.

## Verify Network Connectivity

Run the following command to confirm connectivity to internal infrastructure.

```bash
ping -c 4 10.0.0.1
# Check network reachability to internal hosts
```

# 4. Review Existing Incidents and Alerts

Before accepting ownership of the shift, review all outstanding incidents from the previous shift.

## Incident Review Checklist

* Log in to **ServiceNow** or **JIRA**.
* Review all active **P1** and **P2** incidents.
* Read the complete incident history and engineer notes.
* Understand the current status before taking ownership.
* Acknowledge all monitoring alerts assigned to your support queue.

### Example ServiceNow Filter

```text
Assignment Group = Linux L2
State = In Progress
```

# ✅ Operational Best Practices

The following best practices should be followed during every shift.

### ⏰ Arrive Early

Arrive **10–15 minutes before your scheduled shift** to receive a complete handover from the outgoing engineer.

---

### 📝 Maintain a Shift Log

Keep a personal shift log using Notepad, OneNote, or your preferred documentation tool.

Record:

* Major incidents
* Troubleshooting activities
* Configuration changes
* Escalations
* Follow-up actions

---

### 🚨 Escalate Critical Incidents

Never manage a **P1 (Critical)** incident independently.

Notify and involve the **Shift Lead within the first 10 minutes** of identifying or receiving the incident.

---

### ⏱️ Monitor SLA Timers

Update the SLA timer immediately after acknowledging the ticket.

> **Important:** SLA measurement begins from the **ticket creation time**, **not** from the acknowledgment time.

---

### 📄 Document in Real Time

Update incident tickets continuously while performing troubleshooting.

Avoid documenting all actions only at the end of the shift, as this can reduce accuracy and delay communication.

---

### ✔️ Follow the Shift Checklist

Use the organization's standard shift checklist at the beginning of every shift.

The checklist may be maintained in either:

* Printed format
* Digital format (OneNote, Wiki, SharePoint, etc.)

Using the checklist consistently helps ensure operational consistency and reduces the risk of missed tasks.
